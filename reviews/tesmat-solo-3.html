Frequently Asked Questions

Can you sleep in a Tesla Model 3 with the TESMAT Solo?

Yes, but the Model 3 is more compact than the Model Y. The TESMAT Solo fits the Model 3's interior perfectly, but people over 5'10" may find it tight. For occasional camping or emergency use, it works well.

Is the TESMAT Solo Model 3 the same as the Model Y version?

The construction is identical - 3-inch gel memory foam with built-in pillow. The only difference is the dimensions, which are custom-cut for the Model 3's specific interior measurements.

Should I get the TESMAT Solo or Luxe for my Model 3?

If budget is your priority and you camp occasionally, the Solo at $139 is fine. If you plan to camp more regularly or want better comfort, invest in the TESMAT Luxe at $379 or the Snuuzu Model 3 for maximum comfort.

Is the gel memory foam CertiPUR-US certified?

Yes, the TESMAT Solo uses CertiPUR-US certified gel memory foam, ensuring it's made without harmful chemicals like formaldehyde or heavy metals. It's safe for sleeping with no off-gassing concerns.

Does the Solo Model 3 need a pump?

No, the TESMAT Solo is a pure foam mattress - just unroll it and you're ready to sleep. No inflation required, making setup incredibly simple.

Does the Solo Model 3 work with the 2024 Highland?

Yes, the TESMAT Solo Model 3 fits all Model 3 years including the 2024 Highland refresh. The cargo area dimensions remain consistent across model years.

How does the built-in pillow work?

The Solo has an integrated pillow section built into one end of the foam. It's raised slightly to support your head without needing a separate pillow. Most users find it adequate for camping.

Can I keep the Solo in my trunk permanently?

Yes, the Solo rolls up compactly and at 14 lbs, it doesn't take up much space. Many owners keep it in the sub-trunk or frunk for spontaneous camping trips or roadside naps.

The Bottom Line

The TESMAT Solo Model 3 is the lowest-cost way to try Tesla camping in your Model 3. At $139, it's a low-risk investment that lets you test the lifestyle without committing $400-900.

The reality is that Model 3 camping is more challenging than Model Y camping due to the smaller interior. Combined with the thinner Solo mattress, this is really best for occasional use, emergency situations, or as a test before upgrading to a better mattress.

If you're serious about camping in your Model 3, consider the TESMAT Luxe Model 3 ($379) for a complete kit, or the Snuuzu Model 3 ($899) for premium comfort. But for testing the waters, the Solo does the job.
